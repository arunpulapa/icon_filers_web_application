W-7 ITIN Application Filing

We prepare and file your W-7 ITIN package end-to-end, making sure all documents and supporting proofs match IRS requirements.

  • Determine whether you qualify for an ITIN and under which category.
  • Prepare Form W-7 with accurate, IRS-ready information.
  • Guide you on identity and foreign status documents to submit.
  • Help you choose the correct method: mail, in-person TAC, or CAA.

ITIN for Nonresident Spouse & Dependents

Add nonresident spouses and dependents correctly so you can file joint returns and claim eligible credits where allowed.

  • Review residency, relationship and support tests for dependents.
  • Prepare separate W-7 applications for each family member.
  • Coordinate W-7s with your 1040 / 1040NR filing strategy.
  • Explain how ITINs impact credits and future filings.

ITIN for Nonresident US Business Owners

Nonresident owners of US LLCs or corporations often need ITINs to meet banking, reporting and tax obligations.

  • Determine ITIN need for ownership, banking or filing reasons.
  • Align W-7 applications with EIN, entity filings and K-1s.
  • Prepare supporting letters and ownership proofs when required.
  • Coordinate timing with initial or first-year tax filings.

ITIN Renewal Application

Renew your ITIN before it delays refunds or rejects your tax return – especially if you haven’t filed for a few years.

  • Check whether your ITIN has expired or is set to expire.
  • Prepare renewal W-7 and required documentation.
  • Advise on timing so current-year returns are not delayed.
  • Update IRS records when your name, address or status changed.

1040 & 1040NR Tax Filing with ITIN

Combine ITIN work with your actual tax filing so both are consistent and processed smoothly by the IRS.

  • Prepare 1040 or 1040NR returns that match your W-7 filings.
  • Attach returns to initial ITIN applications when required.
  • Handle joint filings, dependents and foreign income correctly.
  • Provide copies and guidance for future-year filings after ITIN approval.
