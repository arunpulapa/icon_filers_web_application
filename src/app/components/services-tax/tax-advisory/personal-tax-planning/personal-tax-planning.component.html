Personal Financial Goals Identification

We start by understanding where you are and where you want to go: family, career, relocation, retirement, education, or business dreams — then align your tax strategy to support those goals.

  • Discuss short, medium and long-term financial goals.
  • Map major life events (marriage, kids, property, relocation).
  • Review income sources: salary, business, rental, investments.
  • Build a high-level tax roadmap for the next 3–5 years.

Tax Return Review

We carefully review your recent tax returns to ensure they are accurate, consistent, and aligned with your goals.

  • Check past 1–3 years of returns for obvious errors or gaps.
  • Compare actual numbers vs. paystubs, 1099s and statements.
  • Confirm filing status, dependents, and residency treatment.
  • Identify areas where documentation should be improved.

Identify Missed Deductions & Credits

We look for money left on the table — deductions and credits you may have been eligible for but didn’t claim.

  • Review housing, education, childcare and medical expenses.
  • Check retirement contributions, HSA/FSA and charity records.
  • Evaluate state-specific credits and benefits you may qualify for.
  • Summarize potential tax savings by year and category.

Cost Benefit Analysis for Amendments

Not every mistake requires an amended return. We calculate whether amending is worth it — in time, fees and potential risk.

  • Estimate additional refund or reduced tax if amended.
  • Compare benefit vs. professional fees and effort.
  • Consider statute-of-limitation deadlines for each year.
  • Provide a clear recommendation: “Amend” or “Monitor only”.

Employer Stock Option Advice

Understand the tax impact of RSUs, ESOPs, ESPP and stock options, and plan exercises and sales in a tax-efficient way.

  • Explain how your specific equity plan is taxed over time.
  • Compare different exercise / sale scenarios before you act.
  • Coordinate with capital gains, AMT exposure and residency.
  • Build a simple action plan for upcoming vesting events.

When Personal Tax Planning Helps Most

  • You expect big changes – new job, relocation, property or sale of assets.
  • You receive RSUs/ESOP/ESPP or other equity compensation.
  • Your tax bill or refund jumped unexpectedly vs. last year.
  • You want a clear, written plan instead of one-time filing only.
