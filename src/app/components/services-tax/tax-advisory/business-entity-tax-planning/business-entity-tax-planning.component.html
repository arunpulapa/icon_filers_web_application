Business Financial Goals Identification

We start with your business vision: growth, profitability, exit plans and cash-flow needs — then design a tax strategy that supports those goals.

  • Clarify short-term and long-term business targets.
  • Map owner cash-flow needs vs. reinvestment plans.
  • Identify tax-sensitive events: acquisitions, exits, equity grants.
  • Build a simple roadmap that connects goals to tax actions.

Tax Return Review & Projections

We review prior returns for issues and build forward-looking projections, so you know your estimated bill before year-end.

  • Check last 1–3 years of business and owner returns.
  • Spot red flags, inconsistent reporting or missed deductions.
  • Create year-end projections based on YTD numbers.
  • Suggest specific moves before 31st Dec to reduce taxes.

Retirement Account Planning

Choose and size the right retirement plan for your entity type and income level – balancing owner benefits with cash-flow.

  • Compare 401(k), Solo 401(k), SEP and SIMPLE IRA options.
  • Calculate maximum deductible contributions for owners.
  • Model impact on current-year tax and future savings.
  • Coordinate plan design with payroll and entity structure.

Payroll Planning for Owners & Family

Optimize how owners and family members are paid – salary, distributions, or contractor payments – while staying compliant.

  • Reasonable salary planning for S-Corp owners.
  • Children and spouse on payroll vs. contractor arrangements.
  • Coordinate W-2 wages with retirement and benefit plans.
  • Estimate payroll tax impact of different scenarios.

Entity Restructuring (LLC vs C-Corp vs S-Corp)

As your business evolves, the original legal structure may no longer be the best fit. We help evaluate and implement changes in a tax-efficient way.

  • Compare tax impact of LLC, C-Corp and S-Corp structures.
  • Model owner take-home pay under each option.
  • Plan timing of elections (e.g. S-Election) and restructures.
  • Coordinate filings and compliance when you change structure.

When Business Tax Planning Makes Sense

  • Your revenue or profit has grown quickly in the last 1–2 years.
  • You’re paying yourself in multiple ways (salary, draws, dividends).
  • You are considering hiring family or adding partners/investors.
  • You’re not sure if your current entity type is still the best fit.
