Residents (Form 1040)

We prepare full-year resident returns covering your salary, investments, dependents and deductions – with a focus on the highest legal refund.

  • W-2 wages, bonuses and RSUs from U.S. employers.
  • Bank interest, dividends and capital gains reporting.
  • Standard vs itemized deduction optimisation.
  • Child Tax Credit, Education credits and other major credits.

Nonresidents (Form 1040NR)

For students, visitors and professionals who are non-U.S. residents for tax purposes, we ensure only U.S. source income is reported and treaty benefits are correctly applied.

  • Residency test and treaty benefit review.
  • Reporting of U.S.-source wages, scholarships and stipends.
  • Correct treatment of exempt income and dependents.
  • Guidance on transitioning from 1040NR to 1040 in future years.

Immigrants & Visa Holders (F1, J1, O1, H1B, EAD)

We specialize in filings for international students and professionals, handling mid-year arrivals, status changes and dual-status situations.

  • F1 / J1 student and researcher income & scholarship reporting.
  • Visa changes (F1 → H1B, J1 → H1B) and dual-status returns.
  • Foreign income and foreign bank account considerations.
  • Clear guidance on what documents to keep for future years.

Salary Income (W-2 Employees)

Perfect for single or multiple W-2 jobs, including remote and hybrid workers who may have income across multiple states.

  • Multiple W-2s from different employers and states.
  • HSA, 401(k), ESPP and stock-based compensation review.
  • State-to-state moves and part-year resident filings.
  • Paycheck review to avoid surprises next tax season.

Contractor Income & Expenses

For freelancers, consultants and gig-workers receiving 1099-NEC or 1099-K, we help you capture every eligible business expense.

  • Schedule C preparation for freelance and self-employment work.
  • Book-keeping support and expense categorisation.
  • Home office, mileage, software and equipment deductions.
  • Quarterly estimated tax guidance to avoid penalties.

How Your Individual Return Is Prepared
  1. We collect your W-2s, 1099s and other tax documents.
  2. Determine correct residency (1040 vs 1040NR) and filing status.
  3. Enter income, deductions & credits to maximize your refund.
  4. Review the return with you and explain your refund or tax due.
  5. E-file the return and share a copy plus next-year planning tips.
  6. Support with basic IRS/state notices related to this filing.
